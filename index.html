What it does

✋

Gestures → keys

Hand position is translated into slope and distance, then mapped to W/A/S/D keypresses via PyAutoGUI — your hand becomes the controller.

👁️

Real computer vision

OpenCV captures the webcam feed, isolates your hand by colour, and tracks its contour frame by frame to read each gesture.

🎚️

Tunable with trackbars

On-screen HSV trackbars let you dial in lighting and skin tone so only your hand is detected — then flip Start to go live.

🏎️

Plays real games

Tested driving through Need for Speed, GTA 5, Blur and Spiderman — and the gesture mapping is yours to customise.

How it works

Camera init. camera.py starts the webcam and hands frames to the pipeline.

Tune HSV. trackbars.py exposes hue/saturation/value sliders so only your hand stays visible against the background.

Process frames. processing.py finds the hand contour in each frame using the tuned colour mask.

Map & press. control.py turns slope and distance into gestures, and directkeys.py fires the matching keypress into the game.
